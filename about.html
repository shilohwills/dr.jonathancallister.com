About Dr. Callister

Dedicated to providing exceptional cancer care with compassion and expertise

Professional Background

With over 15 years of specialized experience in oncology, Dr. Maya Thompson has dedicated her career to advancing cancer treatment and providing exceptional care to patients facing complex diagnoses. She specializes in breast, lung, and colorectal cancers, utilizing the latest evidence-based approaches to develop personalized treatment plans.

Dr. Thompson earned her medical degree from Harvard Medical School with honors, followed by residency at Johns Hopkins Hospital. She completed her fellowship in Medical Oncology at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center, where she developed expertise in targeted therapies and immunotherapy approaches.

Treatment Philosophy

Dr. Thompson believes in treating the whole person, not just the disease. Her approach combines cutting-edge medical science with genuine compassion, ensuring each patient receives care tailored to their unique needs. She emphasizes clear communication, empowering patients with knowledge about their condition and involving them in every decision.

"Cancer treatment isn't just about fighting disease—it's about preserving quality of life, maintaining dignity, and supporting patients through every step of their journey," says Dr. Thompson.

A Personal Commitment

Understanding the human side of cancer care

Why Oncology?

"My journey into oncology began when I was just 14 years old. My grandmother was diagnosed with advanced breast cancer, and I watched how her oncologist not only treated her disease but also cared for her spirit. That physician showed me that cancer treatment is both a science and an art—one that requires equal measures of technical expertise and human compassion."

"I consider it the greatest privilege to walk alongside my patients during their most challenging moments. My role isn't just to treat cancer—it's to help people find hope, maintain their identity beyond diagnosis, and live fully through treatment and beyond."

Dr. Jonathan M. Callister

Oncology Specialist

Outside the clinic, Dr. Thompson volunteers with cancer support organizations and mentors medical students interested in oncology. She's an advocate for patient-centered care reforms and speaks nationally about improving communication in cancer treatment.
