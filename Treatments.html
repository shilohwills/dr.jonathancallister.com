Cancer Treatment Options

Personalized care plans designed with compassion, innovation, and hope

Your Journey to Healing Starts Here

Welcome to our comprehensive guide to cancer treatment options. As your oncology team, we understand that a cancer diagnosis can feel overwhelming. Our approach combines cutting-edge medical science with compassionate care tailored to your unique situation.

We believe in treating the whole person, not just the disease. Your treatment plan will be carefully crafted based on your specific cancer type, stage, overall health, and personal preferences. We're here to guide you through every step of your journey with expertise and empathy.

Our Treatment Options

We offer a comprehensive range of evidence-based cancer treatments

Chemotherapy

Chemotherapy uses powerful medications to destroy cancer cells throughout the body. It can be administered orally, intravenously, or through injections.

How it's used:

Often used as primary treatment, before surgery to shrink tumors, after surgery to eliminate remaining cells, or for advanced cancers to control symptoms.

Common side effects:

  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Hair loss
  • Increased infection risk

How we manage side effects:

We provide advanced anti-nausea medications, nutritional counseling, scalp cooling to reduce hair loss, and supportive therapies to maintain your quality of life.

Radiation Therapy

Radiation therapy uses high-energy particles or waves to destroy or damage cancer cells. It precisely targets tumors while sparing healthy tissue.

Technologies we use:

  • Intensity-modulated radiation therapy (IMRT)
  • Stereotactic body radiotherapy (SBRT)
  • Image-guided radiation therapy (IGRT)
  • Brachytherapy (internal radiation)

Effectiveness:

Radiation can cure early-stage cancers, prevent recurrence after surgery, shrink tumors to relieve symptoms, and be combined with other treatments for enhanced effectiveness.

Side effects management:

We minimize side effects through precise targeting, skin care protocols, dietary guidance, and fatigue management strategies.

Immunotherapy

Immunotherapy harnesses your body's immune system to recognize and destroy cancer cells. It works by boosting your natural defenses or training them to target cancer.

How it boosts immunity:

  • Checkpoint inhibitors to "release the brakes" on immune cells
  • CAR T-cell therapy to enhance your T-cells' cancer-fighting ability
  • Cancer vaccines to stimulate immune response

Effective for:

Melanoma, lung cancer, kidney cancer, bladder cancer, Hodgkin lymphoma, and increasingly for many other cancer types, especially when traditional treatments have limited effectiveness.

Potential side effects:

Fatigue, skin reactions, flu-like symptoms, and autoimmune reactions which we carefully monitor and manage.

Targeted Therapy

Targeted therapy uses drugs designed to specifically attack cancer cells with certain genetic mutations or characteristics, while minimizing damage to healthy cells.

Precision medicine approach:

We use comprehensive genomic testing to identify specific mutations driving your cancer's growth, then select medications that precisely target these vulnerabilities.

How it works:

  • Blocks signals that tell cancer cells to grow
  • Alters proteins inside cancer cells
  • Stops new blood vessels from forming around tumors
  • Triggers cancer cell death

Surgery

Surgical procedures remove cancerous tissue and nearby lymph nodes. It's often combined with other treatments for comprehensive cancer care.

Types of procedures:

  • Curative surgery to remove tumors
  • Debulking surgery to remove part of a tumor
  • Palliative surgery to relieve symptoms
  • Reconstructive surgery after tumor removal

Minimally invasive options:

We specialize in laparoscopic, robotic-assisted, and endoscopic procedures that use smaller incisions, reduce pain, and speed recovery.

Recovery support:

Our team provides comprehensive rehabilitation, pain management, and emotional support throughout your recovery journey.

Clinical Trials

Clinical trials offer access to promising new treatments before they're widely available, contributing to medical progress while potentially benefiting your care.

Opportunities for patients:

  • Access to cutting-edge therapies
  • Close monitoring by expert teams
  • Contribution to cancer research
  • Potential for improved outcomes

Our research involvement:

As active researchers, we participate in national and international clinical trials, bringing innovative options to our patients while advancing cancer science.

Supportive & Palliative Care

Specialized medical care focused on providing relief from symptoms, pain, and stress of serious illness, improving quality of life for both patient and family.

Comprehensive support:

  • Advanced pain management techniques
  • Emotional and psychological support
  • Nutritional counseling
  • Symptom management for treatment side effects

Integrated approach:

Provided alongside curative treatments from diagnosis onward, our supportive care team works with your oncology team to address physical, emotional, and spiritual needs.

Common Questions

Answers to frequently asked questions about cancer treatment

Will I lose my hair during treatment?

Hair loss depends on the specific treatment. While chemotherapy often causes hair loss, many newer treatments do not. We offer scalp cooling technology that can significantly reduce hair loss during chemotherapy. Even if hair loss occurs, it's typically temporary, and we'll provide resources for wigs, head coverings, and support for this transition.

Can I continue working during treatment?

Many patients continue working during treatment, depending on their job demands and treatment intensity. We'll help you develop a schedule that balances treatment with your professional life. Options include adjusting treatment timing, flexible work arrangements, and managing side effects to maintain energy. We also provide documentation for workplace accommodations when needed.

How will treatment affect my daily life?

Modern cancer treatments are designed to minimize disruption to your life. While some treatments may cause fatigue or require recovery time, many patients maintain active lifestyles. We focus on preserving your quality of life through symptom management, scheduling flexibility, and supportive therapies. Our team will help you anticipate adjustments and develop strategies to maintain normalcy.

Are there options to preserve fertility?

Yes, fertility preservation is an important consideration. Before treatment begins, we'll discuss options like egg/sperm freezing, embryo freezing, or ovarian suppression. We collaborate with fertility specialists to provide these services promptly. The timing and options depend on your specific situation, and we'll guide you through the decision-making process.

What support is available for family members?

We offer comprehensive support for families, including counseling, support groups, educational resources, and caregiver training. Our family support program helps loved ones understand the treatment process, manage stress, and provide effective care. We also connect families with community resources for practical assistance with transportation, meals, and financial concerns.
